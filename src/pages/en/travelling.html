Travelling

Party place address: ‘Caryca’ Pub, Dajwór 16 Street, Cracow (Kazimierz district), Poland — see on Google Maps.

You can get the party-place:

  • by car — if you come from the west or east, choose the A4 motorway. Motorways in Poland aren’t free. If you travel from the west, you’ll have to pay for Wroclaw-Katowice and Katowice-Cracow sections (around €4 each, one way). Payments are received at the toll gates, no vignette system. The Cracow ring road section and A4 eastwards from there is free of charge. You can park your car in Kazimierz district, there are multiple watched car parks there, two directly next and opposite the party place (Dajwór street). Prices vary, usually €10-15 a night.
  • by plane — there’s an airport directly in Cracow (not a remote airfield in the middle of nowhere) and the transfer from there to the centre by bus (lines: 208, 292, 902), taxi and other means (private bus service, etc.). We are going to publish a detailed description of this route on our Facebook page so watch that space. You can also fly to Katowice-Pyrzowice (approx. 100 km, ~2h by bus to Cracow) or Warsaw-Okęcie (approx. 300 km, ~3h by train to Cracow). If you need any assistance with travelling, contact us!
  • by train — search for local train connections here.

Accommodation

You have the following options:

  • at the party place — included in the entrance ticket. There are several sofas and an area where you can rest but it will be noisy (as it is in the same room), so earplugs are advised.
  • near the party place — literally hundreds of hotels, hostels and apartments in the city. We do not advertise any of them but if you have problems finding accommodation, we can help.
